The A-10 Thunderbolt II, commonly known as the "Warthog," is a ground attack aircraft developed by Fairchild Republic for the United States Air Force (USAF). Renowned for its durability, firepower, and close air support capabilities, the A-10 has played a significant role in various conflicts since its introduction.

Main Weapon: The A-10's most notable feature is its 30mm GAU-8/A Avenger rotary cannon. This powerful cannon can fire up to 3,900 rounds of depleted uranium per minute and is capable of destroying tanks and other armored vehicles.

Missiles and Bombs: The aircraft can carry a variety of munitions, including AGM-65 Maverick missiles, AIM-9 Sidewinder missiles, and a range of bombs, such as Mk 82 and Mk 84, as well as precision-guided munitions.

Engines: It is powered by two General Electric TF34-GE-100 turbofan engines mounted high on the fuselage to protect them from ground fire and debris.

Speed and Range: The A-10 has a top speed of approximately 420 miles per hour and a combat radius of around 290 miles with loitering capability over the battlefield.
